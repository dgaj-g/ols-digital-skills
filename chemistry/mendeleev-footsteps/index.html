Hello! We're the Young Bunsens. We'll guide you through Mendeleev's discovery, one step at a time.

In the late 1800s, chemists knew of around 60 elements.

They were sure there was a pattern connecting them — but no one had found it yet.

A Russian chemist named Dmitri Ivanovich Mendeleev took up the search. In four steps, you'll follow his footsteps and discover the pattern for yourself.
