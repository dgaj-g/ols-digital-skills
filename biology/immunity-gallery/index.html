Exhibit III

Memory & the Curve

Tap a numbered point on the graph to read what happens there.

Immunity

Above the threshold

You are immune when your antibody level is high enough — or can be made quickly enough — to pass the immunity threshold if the microbe returns. Choose a graph above to watch the antibody level change over time.

Active

The body makes its own antibodies (from infection or a vaccine). Slow to act, but long-lasting. Memory lymphocytes are made.

Passive

Ready-made antibodies are injected. Fast-acting, but short-lived — they are used up and not replaced, and no memory lymphocytes are made.
